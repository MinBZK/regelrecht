---

**Status:** Draft
**Date:** 2026-06-08
**Authors:** Tim de Jager
**Depends on:** RFC-001 (YAML Schema), RFC-003 (IoC for Delegated Legislation), RFC-007 (Cross-Law Execution Model)

## Context

A law version in regelrecht carries a `valid_from` (optional) and a `publication_date`
([RFC-001](/rfcs/rfc-001) §5). There is **no end date**, and version selection
([RFC-003](/rfcs/rfc-003), "Temporal model for open term resolution") filters on the **lower bound
only** (`valid_from <= calculation_date`, newest wins). The consequence: **the most recent version
of any law is treated as valid indefinitely.** A law can only "end" by being superseded - never by
expiring or being repealed.

This is wrong for real Dutch law, where a law can be **terminated** - it *vervalt* or is
*ingetrokken* - with no later version of the same law to follow, so the engine wrongly treats the
last version as valid indefinitely:

- **Self-termination (horizonbepaling).** The Tijdelijke wet maatregelen covid-19 (BWBR0044337) had a
  sunset clause (art. VIII lid 1: provisions *vervallen* three months after entry into force unless
  extended; lid 3 governs extension). When the goedkeuringswet for the fifth extension was rejected
  (Eerste Kamer, 17 mei 2022, dossier 36.042), the measures *vervielen* per 20 mei 2022 - last day in
  force 19 mei 2022.
- **Termination by another instrument.** The Wet op de openluchtrecreatie (BWBR0006548) was
  *gefaseerd ingetrokken* per 1 januari 2008 (responsibility moved to municipalities)
  ([Eerste Kamer 29.829](https://www.eerstekamer.nl/wetsvoorstel/29829_gefaseerde_intrekking_wet_op)).

**Dependents break too ("verweesde regelingen").** When a delegating regulation is repealed, the
regulations resting on it lose their grondslag and can no longer have effect. The old Aanwijzing 243
(pre-2018 numbering) stated this as *van rechtswege vervallen*; its successor, Aanwijzing 6.24, since
2018 prescribes **explicit mede-intrekking** precisely because the van-rechtswege doctrine caused
unclarity (its toelichting still acknowledges the underlying grondslag loss). A real law following
6.24 to the letter is Stb. 2018, 362: one besluit drops the delegation basis (art. 2: "*Artikel 76
van de Gezondheids- en welzijnswet voor dieren vervalt met ingang van 1 november 2018*") and in the
same instrument explicitly repeals the regulations resting on it (art. 3: "*Het Fokkerijbesluit en de
Fokkerijregeling worden ingetrokken met ingang van 1 november 2018*"), its nota van toelichting
citing the rule: "*Deze expliciete intrekking volgt uit aanwijzing 6.24 van de Aanwijzingen voor de
regelgeving.*" The Tijdelijke regeling
maatregelen covid-19 (BWBR0044416) lost its grondslag when the Twm lapsed and was *vervallen* per the
same date (20 mei 2022).
In regelrecht's `source.regulation` model ([RFC-007](/rfcs/rfc-007), [RFC-001](/rfcs/rfc-001) §9)
this is a dangling cross-law reference: the engine would still return the repealed law and compute on
no-longer-valid law - a **silent wrong answer**.

This RFC adds the end date and the basic handling of a reference to an ended law. A reference to a
*changed or ended* law is not always dead - eerbiedigende werking or a statische verwijzing can keep
an earlier version applicable. That nuance, and resolving a reference at a date other than the global
`calculation_date`, is the subject of **RFC-020 (date-aware reference resolution)**. RFC-019 covers
only the case at the global calculation date.

### Scope note: instrument validity ≠ substantive scope

`valid_from`/`valid_to` describe **instrument validity** - when the *text* is in force. This is
distinct from the **substantive scope** a norm governs (e.g. a 2023-only price cap), which is
expressed in the rule's own conditions/parameters and field `temporal` metadata
([RFC-001](/rfcs/rfc-001) §8). RFC-019 only adds instrument validity; it does not infer substantive
scope.

## Decision

### 1. `valid_to`: instrument end date

Add an optional law-level `valid_to`, mirroring `valid_from` ([RFC-001](/rfcs/rfc-001) §5):

```yaml
$id: subsidieregeling_bekostiging_plafond_energietarieven_kleinverbruikers_2023
bwb_id: BWBR0047628
valid_from: '2022-12-15'
valid_to: '2026-12-31'    # art. 8.1 lid 2: "vervalt met ingang van 1 januari 2027" → laatst van kracht 31 dec 2026
```

**Inclusive bound + "met ingang van" conversion.** `valid_to` is the **last calendar day on which
the law is in force** (inclusive), consistent with `valid_from` and with BWB's `einddatum`
("geldend t/m"). A statutory clause "*vervalt met ingang van D*" is therefore **exclusive** and maps
to `valid_to = D − 1`.

**Semantics.** `valid_to` records that the law (this `$id`) is **terminated** - it *vervalt* or is
*ingetrokken*. It is **not** about whether a successor exists; it is about the law being actively
ended. So it is **not** set merely because the content changes: ordinary succession of the same
`$id` is handled implicitly by the next version's `valid_from` (newest-wins,
[RFC-003](/rfcs/rfc-003)). Conversely, a law that is repealed **and** replaced by a *different* law
(a new `$id`) still carries `valid_to` - the old law was terminated, and a different-`$id` successor
does not end it via newest-wins. Setting `valid_to` on every version is rejected (see Alternatives).

**Where the termination date comes from - two provenance modes.**

- **Intrinsic** - a horizonbepaling/vervalbepaling in the law's *own* text ("*deze regeling vervalt
  met ingang van …*"). The date is a property of the law and is grounded in its own article. This is
  what a literal `valid_to` expresses, and is the only mode RFC-019 implements.
- **Extrinsic** - the law is ended by *another* instrument: an intrekkingswet (e.g. the Twm
  verlengings-KBs under art. VIII lid 3, where parts not extended vervielen), or a date set by a
  later KB under a vervalt-bij-KB clause (e.g. Wet open overheid art. 10.2f lid 1: "*Hoofdstuk 6
  vervalt bij koninklijk besluit*"). Then the authoritative end date lives in **that**
  instrument, and the target's end
  should be **derived** from it rather than copied onto the target - the same single-source-of-truth,
  unilateral pattern as lex-specialis `overrides` ([RFC-007](/rfcs/rfc-007)): the terminating law
  declares the termination, the target need not know. A cross-law "terminates" mechanism that derives
  `valid_to` from the terminating instrument is **future work** (a natural extension of
  [RFC-007](/rfcs/rfc-007)); until then an externally-terminated law may carry a literal `valid_to`
  as a denormalised value, with the terminating instrument as the source of record.

**Boundary: validity is static metadata in RFC-019.** Both the engine's version selection and this
RFC treat `valid_from`/`valid_to` as **static fields** read off the loaded law - *no law is executed*
to decide whether another law is in force. The extrinsic mode above, and end dates that are
themselves open ("*Hoofdstuk 6 vervalt bij koninklijk besluit*", Wet open
overheid art. 10.2f lid 1), would make in-force status a **resolved/executed property** rather than static
metadata. That is a deliberate architectural boundary RFC-019 does **not** cross: it has an ordering
subtlety (version selection runs *before* execution, yet deriving validity would require resolving
another law to decide which version is even selectable). Making validity an execution-time concern is
future work, consistent with how `overrides`/IoC already resolve *values* across laws
([RFC-007](/rfcs/rfc-007), [RFC-003](/rfcs/rfc-003)) - but applied to *validity itself*.

**Harvester signal.** BWB publishes an `einddatum` per consolidation, but for ordinary succession
that is merely the day before the next consolidation starts. The genuine-end signal is **the final
consolidation (no later one exists) carrying a finite `einddatum` ≠ the `9999-12-31` sentinel**. The
harvester reads `einddatum` today and discards it; it should write `valid_to` exactly in that case.
(Origin/main PR #764 already skips laws with no consolidated text, so the harvester is the right
layer.)

### 2. Selection gains an upper bound

Extend [RFC-003](/rfcs/rfc-003)'s temporal filter. The rule, precisely ordered:

1. Among versions with `valid_from <= calculation_date`, select the newest (unchanged).
2. If the selected version has a `valid_to` and `calculation_date > valid_to`, the law is **not in
   force** → return nothing. **Do not fall through to an older version** - a repealed law does not
   resurrect a prior version.

In force iff `valid_from <= calculation_date <= valid_to` (inclusive). Additive: laws without
`valid_to` behave exactly as today.

### 3. Expired references: behaviour and honest diagnostics

A cross-law reference whose target is not in force at the `calculation_date` resolves to nothing, so
the dependent does not silently compute on dead law. The engine must distinguish *why* and **report
only what the data proves** ([RFC-015](/rfcs/rfc-015): the engine must not invent legal conclusions):

| Cause | Trace fact (the engine may state) | Must NOT state |
|---|---|---|
| `$id` absent from corpus | "regeling `<id>` niet gevonden" | "geen grondslag" |
| all versions `valid_from > calculation_date` | "geen versie van `<id>` van kracht op `<datum>` (nog niet in werking)" | - |
| selected version `valid_to < calculation_date` | "geen versie van `<id>` van kracht op `<datum>`; laatst geldig t/m `<valid_to>`" | "vervallen zonder opvolger" |

We never assert "geen grondslag / zonder opvolger": eerbiedigende werking, a statische verwijzing, or
an alternative grondslag may keep the law alive (→ RFC-020). The resolver therefore exposes the
*reason* (`NotFound` / `NotYetInForce` / `EndedOn(date)`), and execution reports the corresponding
fact as a typed error (`LawNotFound` / `LawNotYetInForce` / `LawEnded`, the latter two carrying the
reference date and `valid_to`). Recording the same fact on the `CrossLawReference` `PathNode`
([RFC-007](/rfcs/rfc-007) trace types; [RFC-013](/rfcs/rfc-013) per-output provenance) applies to the
tolerated (optional-reference) path below.

**Fatal vs. continue.** Consistent with [RFC-012](/rfcs/rfc-012) (fail-fast default, opt-in
tolerance), an unresolved required reference is an error by default. A reference (or open term, cf.
`required: false` in [RFC-003](/rfcs/rfc-003)) may be declared optional, in which case an ended/
missing target yields a null/taint that propagates like [RFC-012](/rfcs/rfc-012)'s `UNTRANSLATABLE`,
and a downstream requirement decides the outcome ("geen recht") instead of crashing.

### 4. Receipt and conformance

- The execution receipt's `loaded_regulations` scope ([RFC-013](/rfcs/rfc-013)) gains `valid_to`
  alongside the existing `valid_from`, recording the full validity window.
- A `valid_to` filtering test joins the **Temporal** conformance level ([RFC-014](/rfcs/rfc-014)),
  which already covers `valid_from` filtering.

## Worked examples

### A. `valid_to` + upper bound - Prijsplafond energie 2023 (CEK23, BWBR0047628)

This example is a **real corpus law**, harvested from BWB and interpreted through
`/law-interpret` + `/law-reverse-validate`:
`corpus/regulation/nl/ministeriele_regeling/subsidieregeling_bekostiging_plafond_energietarieven_kleinverbruikers_2023/2022-12-15.yaml`.
The genuinely computable, **grounded** content is the plafondtarief (art. 2.2) and volumeplafond
(art. 2.3) - each output is a value the article literally "vaststelt". The instrument end date comes
from art. 8.1 lid 2.

```yaml
$id: subsidieregeling_bekostiging_plafond_energietarieven_kleinverbruikers_2023
regulatory_layer: MINISTERIELE_REGELING
bwb_id: BWBR0047628
valid_from: '2022-12-15'
valid_to: '2026-12-31'          # art. 8.1 lid 2: "vervalt met ingang van 1 januari 2027"
                                # (exclusive) → laatst van kracht 31 december 2026 (inclusive)
articles:
  - number: '2.2'               # plafondtarief - "wordt vastgesteld op: € 0,24755 per kWh" (lid 1) etc.
    machine_readable:
      execution:
        # euro-per-unit rates (EUR/kWh, EUR/m3(n), EUR/GJ), typed `number`: the corpus
        # `amount` convention is whole eurocents and type_spec has no tariff units yet
        output:
          - { name: plafondtarief_elektriciteit, type: number }
          - { name: plafondtarief_gas, type: number }
          - { name: plafondtarief_warmte, type: number }
        actions:
          - { output: plafondtarief_elektriciteit, value: 0.24755 }
          - { output: plafondtarief_gas, value: 0.85734 }
          - { output: plafondtarief_warmte, value: 47.38 }
  - number: '2.3'               # volumeplafond - "wordt vastgesteld op: 2.900 kWh ... per jaar" etc.
    machine_readable:
      execution:
        output:
          - { name: volumeplafond_elektriciteit, type: number }
          # ... gas (1200), warmte (37.0)
  - number: '8.1'               # slotbepaling
    machine_readable:
      execution:
        output:
          - { name: vervaldatum, type: date }   # lid 2: "vervalt met ingang van 1 januari 2027"
        actions:
          - { output: vervaldatum, value: '2027-01-01' }
```

**End date is grounded in art. 8.1.** The full text of lid 2 reads: "*Deze regeling vervalt met
ingang van 1 januari 2027, met dien verstande dat zij van toepassing blijft op subsidies die voor
die datum zijn verstrekt.*" The vervaldatum is in the law's own text, so art. 8.1 outputs it
verbatim (`2027-01-01`) - the grounded source. The top-level `valid_to` (`2026-12-31`) is the
**denormalised** inclusive last-day-in-force derived from it (vervaldatum − 1 day). Today version
selection reads the static `valid_to`; deriving it automatically from the article output is the
"validity as an executed property" boundary discussed in §1.

**The staartzin is eerbiedigende werking - the RFC-020 bridge case.** The proviso "*blijft van
toepassing op subsidies die voor die datum zijn verstrekt*" means the regeling remains applicable
after its instrument end to subsidies granted before it. `valid_to: '2026-12-31'` correctly records
the *instrument* end (the regeling vervalt); §3's honest diagnostics then ensure the engine states
only "no version in force on `<datum>`" - never "no longer applicable", which would contradict the
staartzin. Actually resolving this regeling for a pre-2027 subsidie at a post-2026 date is exactly
RFC-020's eerbiedigende werking / `as_of` case; CEK23 is the canonical real-corpus test for it.

Verified end-to-end with the `evaluate` binary:
- `date: 2023-06-01` → `plafondtarief_elektriciteit: 0.24755`, `volumeplafond_elektriciteit: 2900`, `vervaldatum: 2027-01-01`, …
- `date: 2027-06-01` (after `valid_to`) → fails with the data fact: no version in force on
  2027-06-01, last in force until 2026-12-31 (§2, §3).

**Faithfulness note.** The regeling is a *subsidie aan leveranciers*: the subsidieontvanger is the
leverancier (art. 2.1 lid 1), not the consumer, and no article computes a consumer-facing "korting".
The supplier subsidy *is* computed by art. 3.1-3.5 (per aansluiting: geleverde hoeveelheid begrensd
op het volumeplafond × (contractueel leveringstarief − plafondtarief), plus TUK, minus
brutomarge-correctie per bijlage III). Those formulas are not modeled here because they require
bijlage data and supplier-level aggregates; modeled are the values arts. 2.2/2.3/8.1 literally
vaststellen. The cap's *substantive* scope (calendar 2023) is separate from the instrument validity
(text in force until 2027); per the scope note, substantive scope belongs in the rule, not in
`valid_to`.

### B. The hard case - dynamic, KB-determined, per-provision end (Twm)

The Tijdelijke wet maatregelen covid-19 shows the **limits** of a static law-level `valid_to`, and
why this is exactly the boundary §1 leaves to future work:

- **The end date is not in the law.** Art. VIII lid 1 only says the inserted Hoofdstuk Va Wpg
  *vervalt* three months after entry into force; lid 3 lets a **koninklijk besluit** extend the
  geldingsduur - *or designated parts of it* - by at most three months at a time ("*steeds ten
  hoogste drie maanden*"). The law was extended several times by KB; when the goedkeuringswet for
  the fifth extension was rejected (Eerste Kamer, 17 mei 2022) the measures lapsed per 20 mei 2022.
  The operative end date is therefore **extrinsic
  and dynamic** - set by the verlengings-KBs and a rejected goedkeuringswet, not by any literal in
  the Twm. Authoring `valid_to: '2022-05-19'` here would be **invented**, not grounded in the Twm's
  own text.
- **The verval is per-provision.** Lid 3 extends *aangewezen onderdelen*; parts not extended lapsed
  separately (e.g. the avondklok earlier). "The law" did not end as one block, so a single law-level
  date is too coarse.

So the Twm needs the extrinsic, execution-derived, sub-law-granularity machinery that RFC-019 does
**not** implement (§1 boundary). The clean, grounded, *intrinsic* case is the CEK23 example above
(art. 8.1 lid 2: one literal vervaldatum for the whole regeling).

### C. Expired dependent - orphaned reference

A regulation R reads an output from law B via `source: { regulation: B, output: … }`. With B carrying
`valid_to`, a calculation at a date after B's end resolves B to nothing → R yields no outcome, and the
execution reports "geen versie van B van kracht op `<datum>`; laatst geldig t/m
`<valid_to>`" - instead of computing on the repealed B. Tested end-to-end in
`features/einddatum.feature` (test laws `test_einddatum` / `test_einddatum_afnemer`).

## Testing

Extends the existing harness rather than inventing one. `features/bezwaartermijn.feature` already runs
a cross-law/date chain on a BESCHIKKING. New cucumber-rs scenarios in `features/einddatum.feature`
(test laws `test_einddatum` / `test_einddatum_afnemer`, mirroring `test_untranslatables`):

1. *valid_to upper bound*: a law with `valid_to` resolves before and on the (inclusive) end date and
   fails with the data fact after it (no fall-through to an older version).
2. *expired cross-reference*: dependent R references ended law B; at a `calculation_date` after B's
   `valid_to`, R yields no outcome and the error states the fact (§3).

Plus unit tests on the selection rule (`SelectionReason` cases) and, as follow-up, a `valid_to`
filtering case in the **Temporal** conformance level ([RFC-014](/rfcs/rfc-014)).

## Why

### Benefits

- **Expresses laws that end.** Repealed/expired laws (Twm, CEK23, WOR) become representable; the
  engine stops treating the latest version as eternal.
- **No silent wrong answers.** A reference to an ended law surfaces honestly instead of computing on
  no-longer-valid law.
- **Small and additive.** One field + an upper bound + an honest diagnostic. Laws without `valid_to`
  are unchanged; date-aware resolution is deferred to RFC-020.

### Tradeoffs

- **Authoring discipline.** `valid_to` must be set *only* for genuine ends, mirroring RFC-003's
  existing convention that implementing regulations carry an explicit `valid_from`.
- **Receipt/conformance churn**: receipt scope and the Temporal level need the new field and a case.

### Alternatives Considered

**`valid_to` on every version (mirroring the next version's `valid_from`).** Rejected on grounds of
**faithfulness**: when a newer version simply takes over, the law text contains *no end date* for the
old version - it is merely superseded by the next version's start date. A `valid_to` added there
would be **invented**, not traceable to any article, violating the principle that every value is
grounded in the legal text. `valid_to` is set only where an actual termination - an intrinsic
vervalbepaling, or an external terminating instrument - supplies the date. (Newest-wins already ends
the old version implicitly, so nothing is lost.)

**Engine infers "repealed/no successor" and reports it.** Rejected: violates
[RFC-015](/rfcs/rfc-015) (zero domain knowledge) and is unprovable - eerbiedigende werking or an
alternative grondslag may keep the law alive. The engine reports data facts only.

**Fall through to an older version once the latest has ended.** Rejected: a repealed law does not
resurrect a prior version; if an earlier version still applies, that is the date-aware case in
RFC-020, expressed explicitly - not an implicit fallback.

### Implementation Notes

- Schema: additive `valid_to` (law level); new minor schema version. Non-breaking. The pattern
  accepts literal dates only (no `#`-references): the engine reads `valid_to` as static
  version-selection metadata (§1 boundary), so a reference there would be silently ignored.
- Engine: `ArticleBasedLaw.valid_to: Option<String>`; extend `select_version_for_date` with the
  inclusive upper bound and the no-fall-through rule; the resolver exposes a typed reason
  (`NotFound` / `NotYetInForce` / `EndedOn(date)`) which the cross-law path maps to the honest
  errors of §3; malformed calculation dates are rejected up front so version selection cannot be
  bypassed; the receipt's `loaded_regulations` records `valid_to` (§4).
- Harvester: write `valid_to` from BWB `einddatum` only for the final consolidation when it is finite
  (≠ `9999-12-31`).
