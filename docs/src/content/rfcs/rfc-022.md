# RFC-022: RegelRecht in de chronolexosfeer — koppeling met Mijn Betaaloverzicht

**Status:** Proposed
**Date:** 2026-05-26
**Authors:** Anne Schuth

## Terminology

This RFC uses the conceptual vocabulary from the [Chronolexografie](https://chronolexografie.nl/) position paper (Achterkant van de Overheid, december 2025) and the wider [Nieuwland — een ontwerp voor een digitale rechtsstaat](https://achterkantvandeoverheid.nl/) program. Key terms:

| Term | Working translation | Definition (paraphrased from the position paper) |
|---|---|---|
| **lexogram** | law-recording | vastlegging van een (mogelijke, toekomstige) wijziging in wet- of regelgeving |
| **decretogram** | decision-recording | vastlegging van een concreet besluit of beschikking bij toepassing van wetgeving |
| **executogram** | execution-recording | vastlegging van daadwerkelijke levering of afhandeling van een zaak of dienst |
| **chronolexocel** (cel) | cell | domein waarin chronolexogrammen worden gemaakt, bewaard en verwerkt |
| **chronolexokroniek** (kroniek) | chronicle | tijdsgeordende reeks van chronolexogrammen binnen één cel |
| **chronolexoreductie** (reductie) | reduction | inherent destructieve activiteit van hergebruik van feiten op een later moment |
| **lexostatus** | rechtstoestand-projectie | rechtstoestand zoals gezien vanuit een bepaald (gevraagd) perspectief |
| **chronolexosfeer** (sfeer) | chronolexosphere | de verzameling van cellen samen |

This RFC also uses **Mijn Betaaloverzicht (MBO)** for the citizen-facing service formerly known as *Vorderingenoverzicht Rijk (VORIJK)*. The name change took effect on 2025-07-10. The technical document standard, the **Financial Claims Information Document (FCID)**, retains its original name.

## Context

Three things are happening in Dutch government IT at the same time, and they have not yet been wired together.

First, RegelRecht turns Dutch law into executable specifications. The corpus today contains 23 regulations across fiscal and social domains. A RegelRecht engine reads a YAML version of a law and produces individual decisions with full execution traces.

Second, [Mijn Betaaloverzicht](https://www.eenoverheidsincasso.nl/onze-dienstverlening/vorderingenoverzicht-rijk) consolidates a citizen's financial obligations across at least 15 government opdrachtgevers. The standard underlying MBO is FCID, an event-sourced document type with four event types (`FinancieleVerplichtingOpgelegd`, `BetalingsverplichtingOpgelegd`, `BetalingsverplichtingIngetrokken`, `BetalingVerwerkt`) and four categories.

Third, the Achterkant van de Overheid denktank has published the Nieuwland design and the Chronolexografie position paper. Together they propose a coherent vocabulary and architecture for *adequaat digitaal vastleggen van de rechtstoestand*: cells, chronicles, three types of chronolexograms, and an explicit separation between registratie and interpretatie. One of the editors (Timen Olthof) works on the Vorderingenoverzicht Rijk; one of the people interviewed for inspiration (Eelco Hotting, BZK) is also driving the conversation that produced this RFC. The conceptual ground is therefore shared, but no implementation has yet sat on it.

This RFC ties the three together. It positions a RegelRecht engine as a **chronolexocel** that produces three distinct kinds of chronolexogram, defines how those chronolexograms surface in FCID, and uses the [RFC-009 (Multi-Organisation Execution)](./rfc-009) federation as the cross-cell transport.

### Why this is necessary

A citizen who receives a Wahv fine today sees an amount in MBO but cannot trace it to the article of the [Wet administratiefrechtelijke handhaving verkeersvoorschriften](https://wetten.overheid.nl/BWBR0004581) that produced it. Conversely, a future RegelRecht regulation that needs the list of openstaande vorderingen for a citizen has no formal way to query CJIB. The keten "burger ontvangt boete → ziet vordering in Mijn Betaaloverzicht → krijgt juridische uitleg → betaalt → ziet betaling terug" falls apart at every transition.

In Chronolexografie-terms: the lexogram (the law), the decretogram (the beschikking imposing the fine), and the executogram (the payment receipt) live in different systems with no shared idiom for crossing the boundary. RegelRecht has lexograms in its corpus and produces decretograms through its engine. MBO publishes executograms (and some decretograms) to citizens. The bridge is the missing piece.

### Design principles

1. **The law is the lexogram.** A RegelRecht corpus entry is a lexogram in the Chronolexografie sense. Successive `valid_from` versions form the lexogram-chronicle for that law.
2. **The engine is a chronolexocel.** Each organization running a RegelRecht engine is a cel that produces decretograms (and, when wired in, emits executograms upstream from its incasso system).
3. **Decretograms and executograms are not the same thing.** A beschikking that imposes a payment obligation is a decision. A payment arriving in the bank account is an execution. Conflating the two in one enum (as the first draft of this RFC did) erases a juridically meaningful distinction.
4. **Federation reuses RFC-009.** Cross-cel transport is FSC. Trust, signing, identity, and trace-id propagation are not reinvented.
5. **Citizens "samen zien".** The citizen-side composition of multiple lexostatuses from multiple cells is exactly what MBO already does for executograms. RegelRecht adds the underlying decretograms and lexograms to that view.

## Decision

### 1. RegelRecht engine as chronolexocel

A RegelRecht engine deployed at an organization (CJIB, Belastingdienst, Dienst Toeslagen, a gemeente) is treated as a chronolexocel under its `competent_authority` identity (see [RFC-009](./rfc-009) §2 on engine identity). The cel manages at least one chronicle per regulation it executes for that organization.

This is a conceptual recognition, not a new code path. RFC-009's engine identity already provides cel-identity. RFC-013 (Execution Provenance, Draft) already provides chronicle-style ordered traces. What this RFC adds is the vocabulary and the explicit distinction between the three chronolexogram types the cel produces.

### 2. Three chronolexogram types in the schema

Each chronolexogram type maps to a different RegelRecht concept. The schema bump described below (§5) introduces the executogram concept; the others already exist in some form.

**Lexogram** — already exists. The YAML file `corpus/regulation/<jurisdiction>/<type>/<name>/<valid_from>.yaml` is the lexogram. The collection of versions for one regulation is the lexogram-chronicle. No new schema needed.

**Decretogram** — engine output with `produces.legal_character: BESCHIKKING`. New `decision_type` values are added for the financial-enforcement domain (§5.1). The execution trace from RFC-013 is the decretogram body. The engine signature from RFC-009 §5 is the decretogram signature.

**Executogram** — a new conceptual slot. An executogram is not a regulation output and does not belong inside an article's `produces`. It is the recording of a factual event (payment received, document delivered, service performed) that the cel learns from its surrounding systems. A new corpus directory `corpus/executogram/` holds executogram-stream definitions: which factual events the cel records, how they map to FCID event types, who the competent authority is. The engine emits these on receipt, signs them, and propagates them through FSC just like decretograms.

This separation is the central design move of this RFC. The first draft conflated `BETALING_VERWERKT` with the decision-type enum. That was wrong: a betaling is a fact, not a besluit. The Chronolexografie distinction makes this explicit and the schema follows.

### 3. The cel emits two FCID surfaces

Both decretograms and executograms surface as FCID events when the cel is configured to emit. The mapping:

| Chronolexogram type | RegelRecht source | FCID `event_type` |
|---|---|---|
| Decretogram | `BESCHIKKING` + `decision_type: STRAFBESCHIKKING` (totaal-bedrag) | `FinancieleVerplichtingOpgelegd` |
| Decretogram | `BESCHIKKING` + `decision_type: BETALINGSVERPLICHTING` | `BetalingsverplichtingOpgelegd` |
| Decretogram | `BESCHIKKING` + `decision_type: BESTUURLIJKE_BOETE` | `BetalingsverplichtingOpgelegd` |
| Decretogram | `BESCHIKKING` + `decision_type: INCASSO_BESCHIKKING` | `BetalingsverplichtingOpgelegd` |
| Decretogram | Override via [RFC-007](./rfc-007) replacing an earlier BESCHIKKING | `BetalingsverplichtingIngetrokken` (for the replaced one) |
| Executogram | Executogram-stream `betaling_ontvangen` | `BetalingVerwerkt` |

Field-level derivation for decretogram emission:

- `juridische_grondslag_omschrijving` ← first sentence of `article.text`, or `article.title` if shorter
- `juridische_grondslag_bron` ← `article.url` (canonical wetten.overheid.nl link)
- `zaakkenmerk` ← deterministic hash of (engine `organisation_id` + beschikking-id)
- `gebeurtenis_kenmerk` ← UUID v7
- `bedrag` ← currency-typed output × 100 (FCID requires centen, integer)
- `signature` ← signed with the same FSC key from [RFC-009 §5](./rfc-009)
- `trace_id` ← propagated from the decretogram's execution trace, following W3C Trace Context

Field-level derivation for executogram emission: the same `zaakkenmerk` as the originating decretogram (linking the payment back to the obligation), a fresh `gebeurtenis_kenmerk`, the factual `bedrag` and timestamp from the source incasso system, and a separate FSC signature from the executogram cel's key (which may or may not be the same key as the decretogram cel).

### 4. Two interaction roles for a cel

A cel that interacts with the chronolexosfeer can play producer, consumer, or both:

- **Producer**: emits decretograms (from its engine) and executograms (from its surrounding systems) as FCID events to MBO and, via FSC, to other cells that have a contract for them.
- **Consumer**: queries other cells' chronolexostreams as input to its own regulations. This is the RFC-009 ACCEPT path with a Chronolexografie-aware label.

The consumer side uses a wrapper regulation `procedureregeling_vorderingenoverzicht_rijk` in `corpus/regulation/`, declaring CJIB as `competent_authority` for the output `openstaande_vorderingen[]`. Any downstream regulation references it via standard `source.regulation`:

```yaml
input:
  - name: openstaande_vorderingen
    source:
      regulation: "procedureregeling_vorderingenoverzicht_rijk"
      output: "openstaande_vorderingen"
      parameters:
        bsn: $bsn
```

The engine resolves through RFC-009's decision tree. A non-CJIB engine hits ACCEPT and calls CJIB's cel via FSC. A CJIB engine executes locally, which delegates to its internal MBO query system. The wrapper does not duplicate FCID semantics; it presents the result as a list of vordering records suitable for downstream RegelRecht inputs.

### 5. Schema changes

This RFC bumps the schema to `v0.6.0` with three changes:

#### 5.1. `produces.decision_type` enum extension (decretograms only)

Add:

- `BETALINGSVERPLICHTING` — algemene financiële verplichting opgelegd door bestuursorgaan
- `STRAFBESCHIKKING` — strafrechtelijke afdoening onder Sv 257a
- `BESTUURLIJKE_BOETE` — sectorale bestuurlijke boete (NVWA, NEa, RDI, ATKM)
- `INCASSO_BESCHIKKING` — expliciet incassobesluit, doorgaans na non-betaling van een onderliggende verplichting
- `INTREKKING_BESCHIKKING` — intrekking of vernietiging van een eerder besluit

`BETALING_VERWERKT` from the first draft is removed from this enum. A payment receipt is not a decision; it is an executogram. See §5.3.

#### 5.2. Optional FCID-emit flags on `produces`

Two new optional fields:

- `fcid_emit: bool` (default `false`) — when true, the engine emits an FCID event mirroring this beschikking
- `fcid_category: enum [ALGEMEEN, ADMINISTRATIEKOSTEN, VERHOGING, RENTE]` — required when `fcid_emit: true`

`legal_character` is unchanged.

#### 5.3. New executogram concept

Introduce a sibling directory to `corpus/regulation/`: `corpus/executogram/`. An executogram-stream file declares what factual events a cel records, who is the competent authority, and how each event maps to FCID:

```yaml
$id: cjib_betalingen
competent_authority: CJIB
events:
  - name: betaling_ontvangen
    source: incasso_system
    fcid:
      event_type: BetalingVerwerkt
      category: ALGEMEEN
    fields:
      zaakkenmerk: $external.zaakkenmerk
      bedrag: $external.bedrag_centen
      gebeurtenis_datetime: $external.received_at
```

A separate JSON schema for executogram files lives at `schema/v0.6.0/executogram.json`. The engine validates executogram files at startup just as it validates regulation files.

This separation respects Chronolexografie's principle that registratie en interpretatie are different acts. The lexogram (regulation YAML) is interpretation. The executogram (event recording) is registratie. Mixing them in one schema would erase the distinction.

### 6. Cell identity, trust, and signing

Identity follows [RFC-009 §2](./rfc-009). The cel is identified by `organisation_id` (OIN), and matches `competent_authority` declarations in lexograms.

Signing follows RFC-009 §5. Both decretograms and executograms are signed with the cel's FSC key. The receiver verifies against the FSC Directory's Trust Anchor.

A subtle point: in Dutch practice, a single organization may run multiple cells. CJIB's strafrechtelijke afdoening (under MinJenV mandate, Boek 6 Sv) is arguably a different cel than its inning-namens-CAK (under separate convenant). The RFC does not prescribe one-cel-per-organization. The engine identity in RFC-009 supports multiple identities per deployment if needed.

### 7. Chronicle and trace

Each cel keeps its own chronicle. Within a cel, chronolexograms are ordered. Across cells there is no global ordering — this matches both Chronolexografie's chronicle model and RFC-009's privacy-by-default trace model (traces stay with the executing org).

When a consumer cel accepts a value from a producer cel, an `AuthorityCall` trace node (RFC-009 §8) records the call. The trace carries the W3C Trace Context `trace_id`. Both cells log their side under the same `trace_id`, neither shares its full chronicle, and the citizen can request their chronicle entries from each cel separately under AVG art. 15 (right of access).

This is the Chronolexografie principle of "respectvol onderscheid tussen registreren en interpreteren" turned into a privacy property: the producer cel's chronicle (the registratie) stays at the producer; the consumer cel's interpretation lives downstream; the citizen sees both via lexostatus queries to each cel.

### 8. Reduction and lexostatus

A RegelRecht-engine execution is in Chronolexografie-terms a **chronolexoreductie**: the cel filters the relevant chronolexograms from its chronicles and combines them via an afleidingsproces (the regulation's machine_readable logic) into a **lexostatus** for a particular citizen at a particular reference date. The "destructive" character of reduction is the same as RegelRecht's execution: only the subset of facts relevant to the requested perspective surfaces in the output.

This is not a new feature. It is a recognition that what RegelRecht already does has a name in the Nieuwland vocabulary, and using that name aligns the two ecosystems.

### 9. "Samen zien" at the citizen surface

In Nieuwland §5.4, "samen zien" is gelijktijdige en gelijkwaardige toegang door burger en overheid tot dezelfde gegevens. MBO already delivers this for executograms (a citizen sees their payments). With this RFC, the same surface also delivers decretograms (the beschikking that imposed the obligation) and lexograms (the article of the law that defined the rule). The citizen and the bronorganisatie see the same chain.

The citizen-facing UI is out of scope for this RFC. What is in scope is that the cel emits the chronolexograms in a form that the MBO portal (or any other lexostatus-consuming surface) can present.

### 10. Out of scope

- **Legal basis for inter-organisation data exchange**. Each FSC contract requires its own statutory basis. RFC-009 already notes this.
- **Citizen authentication**. DigiD-binding on the consumer side is a separate concern at the API gateway layer.
- **Payment processing**. iDEAL, automatic incasso, reconciliation — out of scope.
- **The Wet gegevensboekhouding** (Nieuwland §7.3.2). This RFC is an architectural step that would *implement* such a law once it exists; it does not propose the law itself.
- **The full VORIJK API surface**. FCID is in scope. The Financial Claim Request API and Session API are referenced for context; their integration is a follow-up RFC.
- **Reconciliation of decretogram and executogram chronicles when they live in different cells of the same organization**. Real CJIB deployments may need this; the RFC does not prescribe how.

## Why

### Benefits

- **Conceptual integrity**. The Chronolexografie distinction between lexogram, decretogram, and executogram is built into the schema, not papered over by a single overloaded enum. Future readers of a corpus YAML or executogram YAML see the type for what it is.
- **End-to-end traceability**. A citizen seeing a Wahv vordering in MBO can follow the link to the article, the execution trace, the resulting beschikking, and (later) the payment receipt — all under one zaakkenmerk and trace_id.
- **No new identity infrastructure**. FSC, identity, signing all come from RFC-009.
- **Aligns with Nieuwland's "samen zien"**. The same lexostatus surface serves the citizen and the bronorganisatie.
- **Consumer side scales by one corpus addition**. Any future regulation needing openstaande vorderingen reuses the wrapper regulation; no per-consumer plumbing.
- **First multi-domain coupling**. RegelRecht has been fiscal/social. Connecting it to the incasso world via CJIB is the first step into strafrecht and bestuursrechtelijke handhaving.

### Tradeoffs

- **The `decision_type` enum extension is breaking for downstream consumers of the enum**. Schema bump to v0.6.0 is mandatory.
- **Introducing `corpus/executogram/` adds a second top-level corpus category**. Tooling (validators, harvesters, the editor) needs to learn about it. The cost is one-time; the conceptual gain is permanent.
- **FCID is still evolving**. The standard is at v4.2.0 as of mei 2026. Vendoring the FCID JSON schema (in a follow-up implementation PR) lets CI detect breakage when a new minor version lands.
- **Several CJIB-domain regulations are not yet in the corpus**. Wahv, Wet OM-afdoening, the sectoral acts behind bestuurlijke boetes still need to be authored. This RFC enables the integration but does not deliver the regulations.
- **The wrapper regulation `procedureregeling_vorderingenoverzicht_rijk` is conceptually a procedural construct, not a wet in the traditional sense**. Acceptable as long as it is clearly labeled. Chronolexografie's notion that a cel may produce a lexostatus from sources other than a wet text gives this room.

### Alternatives Considered

**Alternative 1: Conflate decretogram and executogram in one `decision_type` enum** (this RFC's first draft).
- Rejected: erases the Chronolexografie distinction. A betaling is a fact, not a besluit. Putting `BETALING_VERWERKT` next to `BESTUURLIJKE_BOETE` in the same enum lies about what kind of recording each is.

**Alternative 2: External microservice transforms RegelRecht output into FCID.**
- Rejected: loses the direct article-to-event link. The article URL needs to travel with the event. A middle transformer re-introduces the provenance gap that this RFC closes.

**Alternative 3: Full structured "incasso-domain" sub-object under `produces`.**
- Rejected: overfits the schema to one external standard. The split between enum extension (decretogram) and separate executogram concept generalizes: another country with another claims standard can map differently without schema surgery.

**Alternative 4: Consumer side only, defer producer to a later RFC.**
- Rejected: the producer side closes the keten for citizens. The driver for this RFC (CJIB engagement, Eelco's "een eerste zetje") is the producer side. Splitting reduces the immediate value.

### Implementation Notes

This RFC is the architecture decision. Schema and engine work are in follow-up PRs so the architecture can be reviewed on its merits.

#### Phase 1: Schema bump

- Bump `schema/v0.5.2` → `schema/v0.6.0`. Update `schema/latest` symlink.
- Add new `decision_type` enum values (decretograms only).
- Add `fcid_emit` and `fcid_category` optional fields under `produces`.
- Add `schema/v0.6.0/executogram.json` for the new executogram-stream file shape.
- No existing corpus regulation needs to change.

#### Phase 2: Producer module

- New: `packages/engine/src/fcid.rs` — pure mapping from decretogram output to FcidEvent struct.
- New: `packages/engine/src/executogram.rs` — loading and validating executogram-stream files, emitting FCID events on external triggers.
- New: `FcidEvent` struct in `packages/engine/src/types.rs`.
- Reuse signing from RFC-009 implementation (`packages/engine/src/signature.rs`).
- New: `packages/editor-api` endpoint for FCID emission from a trace.

#### Phase 3: Schema vendoring and validation

- Vendor FCID v4.x JSON schemas under `schema/external/vorijk/`, with a README documenting upstream source and snapshot date.
- Snapshot tests: a fixture Wahv beschikking, run through `fcid.rs`, validated against the vendored schema.

#### Phase 4: Consumer wrapper and corpus addition

- New: `corpus/regulation/nl/procedureregeling/vorderingenoverzicht_rijk/2026-01-01.yaml`.
- Stub CJIB peer entry in development `service-registry.yaml`.
- BDD scenario in `features/` exercising a hypothetical Wsnp evaluation with mock vorderingen returned by the stub CJIB peer.

#### Phase 5: Executogram pilot

- First executogram-stream file under `corpus/executogram/`, e.g. `cjib_wahv_betalingen.yaml`.
- Engine wiring: external trigger from a mock incasso system → executogram emission → FCID `BetalingVerwerkt`.
