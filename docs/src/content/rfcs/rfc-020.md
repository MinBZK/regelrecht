title: "RFC-020: Article Repeal as Version Succession"
---

**Status:** Draft
**Date:** 2026-06-11
**Authors:** Tim de Jager
**Depends on:** RFC-001 (YAML Schema), RFC-003 (IoC for Delegated Legislation), RFC-019 (Law End Dates)

## Context

[RFC-019](/rfcs/rfc-019) added the **instrument end**: a law-level `valid_to` for the case where a
whole regulation stops existing (horizonbepaling or intrekking). That deliberately leaves a more
common event undescribed: **a single article is repealed while the law lives on**. Stb. 2018, 362
is the canonical pair - art. 3 repeals two whole regulations (the RFC-019 case), while art. 2 only
repeals one article of a law that continues:

> "Artikel 76 van de Gezondheids- en welzijnswet voor dieren vervalt met ingang van
> 1 november 2018." (Stb. 2018, 362, art. 2)

There is no article-level `valid_to` in the schema, and the question came up in review whether one
is needed. This RFC records the decision that it is not: **article repeal is ordinary version
succession**, fully expressed by machinery that already exists. The RFC documents that model
explicitly so it is a decision, not an accident.

## Decision

**A repealed article is represented by a new version of the same law, never by article-level
validity metadata.**

1. **One law, many versions.** A law (`$id`) is stored as one YAML file per consolidation date:
   `corpus/regulation/<layer>/<law>/<valid_from>.yaml` ([RFC-001](/rfcs/rfc-001) §5; e.g.
   `wet_op_de_zorgtoeslag/2024-01-01.yaml` and `2025-01-01.yaml`). Any amendment - including the
   repeal of an article - produces a **new version file with the amendment date as `valid_from`**.
2. **The repealed article disappears from the new version's executable surface.** BWB keeps the
   article as a placeholder in later consolidations (`<artikel label="Artikel 76"><al>Vervallen</al>`,
   a convention the harvester's splitting already handles); it carries no `machine_readable`
   content, so its outputs cease to exist from that version on.
3. **Selection does the rest.** Newest-wins on `valid_from` ([RFC-003](/rfcs/rfc-003)) serves the
   pre-repeal version for dates before the repeal and the post-repeal version after it; the
   inclusive upper bound of [RFC-019](/rfcs/rfc-019) is not involved because the *instrument* never
   ended. Cross-law references resolve against the version in force at the reference date, so a
   dependent reading a since-repealed output still computes correctly for historical dates.
4. **Asking for a repealed output at a post-repeal date is an honest data fact.** The selected
   version does not produce the output, so execution fails with `OutputNotFound` ("Output 'x' not
   found in law 'y'") - the data fact, not a legal verdict, consistent with
   [RFC-019](/rfcs/rfc-019) §3.

### Worked example - art. 76 Gwwd (Stb. 2018, 362)

- BWB publishes a new consolidation of the Gezondheids- en welzijnswet voor dieren with
  inwerkingtreding 2018-11-01 in which artikel 76 is "Vervallen".
- The harvester emits `gezondheids_en_welzijnswet_voor_dieren/2018-11-01.yaml` (same `$id`,
  `valid_from: '2018-11-01'`); the prior version file keeps the article with its full text.
- `calculation_date: 2018-10-01` → the older version, art. 76 executable as before.
  `calculation_date: 2018-12-01` → the 2018-11-01 version; an art. 76 output fails with
  `OutputNotFound`.
- No `valid_to` appears anywhere in this story. The Gwwd only acquired one when the *whole law* was
  later repealed (vervallen per 21-04-2021) - the [RFC-019](/rfcs/rfc-019) event.

### Boundary

What this RFC does **not** cover:

- **Instrument end** - the whole law stops existing → law-level `valid_to`
  ([RFC-019](/rfcs/rfc-019)).
- **Resolving an earlier version on purpose** (statische verwijzing, eerbiedigende werking, ex
  tunc) - that is date-aware resolution (**RFC-022**, `as_of`), which composes with this model: an
  `as_of` before the repeal date reaches the article's last executable version.
- **Per-provision termination of the instrument's own validity** (the Twm case,
  [RFC-019](/rfcs/rfc-019) example B): there the law's *inserted provisions elsewhere* lapsed on
  KB-determined, per-provision dates. As far as the target law's text is concerned this still
  materialises as consolidations (succession); what stays out of scope is giving the Twm itself a
  grounded end date.

## Why

### Benefits

- **Nothing new to build.** Versioning, the harvester's per-consolidation output, newest-wins
  selection and the `OutputNotFound` fact all exist today; this RFC pins the semantics down.
- **Faithful.** The law's own text after the repeal *is* the consolidation without the article. No
  invented per-article dates: the repeal date lives where it legally lives - in the amending
  instrument and in the succession of consolidations.
- **One mechanism for all amendments.** Repeal is not special: renumbering, rewording and repeal
  are all "new version of the same `$id`".

### Tradeoffs

- **Corpus completeness matters.** Historical correctness requires the pre-repeal version files to
  be present in the corpus; a corpus holding only the latest consolidation can not answer
  historical dates. (Operationally: harvest the relevant earlier consolidations, not only the
  newest.)
- **Diagnostic granularity.** `OutputNotFound` does not say "this output existed until
  2018-10-31". A richer message would require scanning older versions; deliberately deferred until
  a real need shows up.

### Alternatives Considered

**Article-level `valid_from`/`valid_to`.** Rejected: it duplicates what version succession already
expresses, and the end date of an article is - like the superseded-version case rejected in
[RFC-019](/rfcs/rfc-019) - not a literal in the law's own text, so authoring it would invent data.
It would also add a second temporal axis to every article lookup for a case selection already
handles.

**Synthesising a new law (`$id`) per amendment.** Rejected: it would break every
`source.regulation` reference and the newest-wins model; versions of one `$id` are the unit of
succession ([RFC-003](/rfcs/rfc-003)).

**Deleting the repealed article from the YAML entirely.** Rejected as a *requirement* (the
executable result is identical), but harvested files follow BWB and keep the "Vervallen"
placeholder - it preserves the article numbering and is itself the faithful text of the
consolidation.

### Implementation Notes

- No engine or schema change. This RFC is documentation of existing, intended behaviour plus the
  boundary to [RFC-019](/rfcs/rfc-019) and RFC-022.
- Conformance: a "repealed article across versions" case fits the **Temporal** level
  ([RFC-014](/rfcs/rfc-014)) next to the `valid_from`/`valid_to` filtering cases.
